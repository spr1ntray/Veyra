## STYLE NOTES

The training dummy is the first opponent/target the player interacts with. The design prioritizes utility and clarity:
- **Utilitarian form**: Simple geometric shapes that clearly represent "thing to hit" — no character personality
- **Damage history**: Subtle burn marks and scars show this dummy has been used extensively without being visually busy
- **Minimal target glow**: The cyan reticle/circle pattern indicates purpose without being distracting from gameplay
- **Static stability**: No dynamic pose — it's a stationary target, the player's mage is the dynamic actor
- **Durability**: Thick simple wooden construction suggests it's built to withstand repeated spellfire
- **Headless/armless**: Dehumanized design (no face, no arms) makes it clear this is a target, not a character or enemy

The dummy should feel like a humble, essential training tool — a practice partner, not an opponent. It has weathered many spells and will weather many more.
